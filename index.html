Why Brussels?

Brussels, the administrative centre of the European Union

Brussels, the capital city of Belgium, is a vibrant and culturally rich metropolis that serves as the de facto capital of the European Union. Nestled in the heart of Western Europe, Brussels is known for its unique blend of French and Dutch influences, creating a distinctive cultural and linguistic diversity. The city is not only a political hub but also a treasure trove of history, art, and culinary delights.

One of the city's most iconic landmarks is the Atomium, a futuristic structure originally built for the 1958 World Expo, symbolizing an iron crystal magnified 165 billion times. The Grand Place, a UNESCO World Heritage site, is another gem that showcases the city's medieval architecture and hosts lively events throughout the year. Brussels is renowned for its culinary scene, offering delectable chocolates, waffles, and, of course, world-famous Belgian fries. Visitors can also explore a plethora of museums, such as the Magritte Museum, dedicated to the surrealist painter Ren√© Magritte, or the Comic Strip Center, celebrating Belgium's comic art heritage.

With its charming cobblestone streets, impressive architecture, and cosmopolitan atmosphere, Brussels invites both tourists and locals alike to immerse themselves in its rich tapestry of history and modernity. Whether strolling through its parks, indulging in its diverse cuisine, or engaging in the dynamic political environment, Brussels captivates with its unique character and welcoming spirit.
